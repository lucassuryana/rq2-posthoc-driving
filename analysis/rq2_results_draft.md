# RQ2 Results — Pilot Study

## Decision Accuracy
Post-hoc access did not improve decision accuracy. Both conditions achieved identical accuracy of 0.417 (10/24), compared to a chance baseline of 0.100. The accuracy difference was 0.000.

## Change Rate
45.8% of samples (11/24) showed action changes when post-hoc information was provided. Changes were not consistently towards the correct answer.

## Semantic Shift
| Step | Mean | Std |
|---|---|---|
| Perception | 0.2443 | 0.1273 |
| Prediction | 0.2070 | 0.0985 |
| Planning | 0.1882 | 0.0669 |
| Reasoning | 0.2099 | 0.0743 |

Perception showed highest shift. Planning showed lowest shift.

## Hypothesis Verdict (H2)
Partially supported. Post-hoc access changed reasoning (change_rate=0.458) but did not improve decisions (Δacc=0.000).

## Limitations
- L1: GT labels from trajectory heuristics
- L2: n=24 insufficient for statistical tests
- L3: CAM_FRONT only
- L4: Images resized to 640x360
- L5: No lane change samples
- L6: Single VLM tested
